## #has_/text_of_/abstract 

> Sir William Wallace (Scottish Gaelic: Uilleam Uallas, pronounced [ˈɯʎam ˈuəl̪ˠəs̪]; 
> Norman French: William le Waleys; c. 1270 – 23 August 1305) 
> was a Scottish knight who became one of the main leaders 
> during the First War of Scottish Independence.
>
> Along with Andrew Moray, 
> Wallace defeated an English army at the Battle of Stirling Bridge in September 1297. 
> 
> He was appointed Guardian of Scotland 
> and served until his defeat at the Battle of Falkirk in July 1298. 
> In August 1305, Wallace was captured in Robroyston, near Glasgow, 
> and handed over to King Edward I of England, who had him hanged, 
> drawn and quartered for high treason and crimes against English civilians.
>
> Since his death, Wallace has obtained a legendary status beyond his homeland. 
> He is the protagonist of Blind Harry's 15th-century epic poem The Wallace 
> and the subject of literary works by Jane Porter and Sir Walter Scott, 
> and of the Academy Award-winning film [[../../../../../../Society/Communication/Media/Movie/Braveheart|Braveheart]]. 
>
> [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/William%20Wallace)
